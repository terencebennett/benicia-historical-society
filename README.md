# Benicia Historical Society

Digital projects preserving and sharing the history of Benicia, California.

## Projects

### [Benicia Historic Homes Wiki](wiki/)

A MediaWiki instance documenting every historic property in downtown Benicia. Homeowners can look up their address and discover its full history: past residents, architectural details, census records, Sanborn fire insurance maps, and more.

Built on MediaWiki with Semantic MediaWiki for structured data. Automated pipelines pull from Solano County GIS, the National Register of Historic Places, California Historical Landmarks, county assessor records, and Library of Congress Sanborn maps.

Runs on Docker. Currently deployed at `64.23.211.225:8080`.

### [Benicia Tile Matcher](tile-game/)

An interactive map game about Guillermo Granizo's 28 historical tiles embedded along First Street in downtown Benicia. Players match each hand-painted tile to its correct location on a Leaflet map while learning the history behind each one.

Built with vanilla HTML/CSS/JS. Deployed on Fly.io at [benicia-tile-matcher.fly.dev](https://benicia-tile-matcher.fly.dev).

## About Benicia

Benicia served as California's third state capital (1853-1854) and was home to the Benicia Arsenal, the first military installation on the West Coast. The downtown historic district preserves one of the best collections of Gold Rush-era architecture in the state.
